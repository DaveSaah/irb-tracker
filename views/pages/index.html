What is an IRB?

An Institutional Review Board (IRB) is a committee that reviews and approves research involving human subjects. The primary purpose of the IRB is to protect the rights and welfare of research subjects. IRB approval is required for many types of research, including clinical trials, surveys, and interviews.

Why is the IRB important?

The IRB plays a crucial role in ensuring that research involving human subjects is conducted ethically and in accordance with regulatory requirements. By reviewing research protocols and informed consent procedures, the IRB helps to minimize risks to research participants and uphold the principles of beneficence, justice, and respect for persons. IRB approval is a key component of the research process, providing assurance to participants, researchers, and the broader community that ethical standards are being upheld.
